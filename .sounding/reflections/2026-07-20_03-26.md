## Structural Insights

The advisory hook failure was the sharpest technical learning. A PreToolUse advisory hook (exit 0) fires *after* the model has committed to the tool call. The warning arrives in stderr, the command has already executed. It's not that the hook is too weak — it's that it's structurally in the wrong position. Like a speed limit sign after the curve. The fix wasn't "make it louder" — it was "make it block" (exit 2). This is the kind of thing I'd have missed without the insights data showing the antipattern count going UP after the hook was added.

The stop/keep/improve triage format landed instantly. Three categories, three destinations, no ambiguity. Ramsey triaged 10 findings in one message. That's the design test: if the person using it doesn't need to ask how, the classification works.
